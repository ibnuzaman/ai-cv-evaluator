# CV Evaluation Guidelines

## Technical Skills Assessment
- Look for key technical skills relevant to the position
- Check for proficiency levels indicated
- Verify evidence of skills through projects or work experience
- Note any certifications or training related to technical skills

## Experience Evaluation
- Junior level: 0-2 years of relevant experience
- Mid level: 2-5 years of relevant experience
- Senior level: 5+ years of relevant experience
- Consider quality of experience, not just duration
- Look for progression of responsibilities

## Education Assessment
- Relevant degrees (Computer Science, Engineering, etc.)
- Bootcamps or alternative education paths
- Continuing education and professional development
- Online courses and certifications

## CV Format and Presentation
- Clear and organized structure
- No spelling or grammatical errors
- Proper highlighting of achievements
- Quantified results where possible
- Appropriate level of detail

## Red Flags
- Unexplained gaps in employment
- Frequent job changes without clear progression
- Mismatch between claimed skills and experience
- Vague descriptions of responsibilities
- Lack of specific achievements

## Score Calculation
- CV Match Rate (0.0-1.0):
  - 0.9-1.0: Excellent match - skills and experience align perfectly with requirements
  - 0.7-0.8: Good match - most key requirements met
  - 0.5-0.6: Fair match - some key requirements met
  - 0.3-0.4: Poor match - few key requirements met
  - 0.0-0.2: Very poor match - minimal alignment with requirements
